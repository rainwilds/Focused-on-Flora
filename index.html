Wallum Phebalium

Wallum Phebalium

The white inflorescence and alternate leaves of the Phebalium (Phebalium woombye). This species can be found in dry heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests from north-east New South Wales to Queensland. P. woombye is a small shrub that can reach a height of 2 m.

The oblong-elliptic leaves are between 2-6cm long. The lower surface of the leaves has silvery scales on them and the leaves are aromatic when crushed. The upper leaf surface is smooth and has a defined channeled midrib. Each inflorescence has between 4-10 flowers and the flower stalks (pedicels) are between 4-15 mm long.

The genus Phebalium is only found in Australia. Most of the species in the genus are obligate seeders and these are species that regenerate from seed following fire. Obligate seeders require time after fire to reach reproductive maturity in order to replenish seed banks. As such, frequent fires can negatively impact their ability to self-perpetuate.
